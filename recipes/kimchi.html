How to Make Kimchi

Korean Spicy Pickled Cabbage

Kimchi is a signature side dish made of fermented cabbage and sometime daikon radishes. It has a sweet, salty, and spicy flavor that goes well with lots of Asian dishes and the probiotics are great for establishing a healthy gut!

Ingredients

  • 1 Large/2-3 smaller napa cabbage (~3lbs)
  • 1 Cup Salt
  • 1 1/2 Cups Water
  • 1/4 Cup Sweet Rice (mochi) flour
  • 1/8 Cup Sugar
  • 1/4 Cup Crushed Garlic
  • 1 Tbsp Ginger, minced
  • 1/4 Cup Onion, minced
  • 1/4 Cup Fish Sauce
  • 1/8 Cup Korean hot pepper flakes (gochugaru) (optional add to preferred taste)
  • 1 Tbps Salt
  • 1/3 Cup Leeks, chopped
  • 2-3 Green Onions
  • 1/4 Cup Carrots, julienned
  • 1/4 Cup Daikon Radish

Instructions

  1. Prep the cabbage soaking in water and salting every 30 min. for a total salting time of 90 min.
  2. Porridge: Bring water and mochi flour to a boil; add sugar. Let cool
  3. Kimchi Paste: Blend porridge, fish sauce, pepper flakes, ginger, garlic, and onion into fine paste
  4. Wash cabbage thoroughly at least 3 times until water runs clear.
  5. Mix cabbage and paste in a large bowl. Suggest using glove and possibly safety goggles.
  6. Place kimchi in airtight glass containers. Keep in fridge at least 3-5 days until you see bubbles forming.
  7. Enjoy!
